Dossier Information (section)
The “Enrolment Status”, “Enrolment Version”, and “Date Last Saved” fields are automatically filled by the form upon selecting “Save Working Copy” or “Save Draft for Filing” in the bottom of the template.
The “Dossier ID” field will be filled by Health Canada when generating final xml file.
The “Company ID” can be obtained from the final company XML file, finalized by Health Canada.

Product name
The brand or proprietary or product name is the name assigned by the manufacturer / sponsor to distinguish the dossier (product) and under which the product is to be sold / advertised. The brand name is also the name used to identify the product in all correspondence related to the submission and on the product label(s) and Product Monograph/Package Insert if applicable. If the brand name has not yet been determined, e.g. CTA, VIND, or NDS submissions, TBD (To be Determined), the proper or common name of the drug or the research code may be used.

Proper, Common or Non-proprietary name
The proper name for a product is the name assigned to the drug in Section C.01.002 of the Food and Drug Regulations, or in boldface type in other Sections of the Regulations or the name of the drug in its finished form identified in the title of a monograph or in any of the official publications listed in Schedule B to the Food and Drugs Act. Example: Acetaminophen, Ferrous Sulphate Tablets.
The common name is the name by which a single ingredient drug is commonly known / designated in scientific or technical journals other than the publications referred to in Schedule B to the Food and Drugs Act. The common name includes the pharmaceutical form when used in relation to the finished drug product.
If there is no proper name and the drug is comprised of a single medicinal ingredient, enter the common name.
If there is no proper name and the drug is comprised of more than one medicinal ingredient, the proper name of the ingredients should be captured and separated by a forward slash (/).For a clinical trial all the active substances should be listed.

REP Contact Information (section)
The Primary REP contact is the person to be contacted by Health Canada if there are any issues or clarifications required for this template when it’s submitted for finalization.
Sponsors may choose to provide an Alternate REP contact, however this is not a requirement.

“Save draft for filing” (button)
Click the "Save Draft for Filing" button to generate the draft XML file.
Submit the created file to Health Canada using the Common Electronic Submission Gateway (CESG).
Refer to the REP guidance document for detailed information.
